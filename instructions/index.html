Marshmallow Challenge Instructions

Step One: Schedule the Challenge

Find about 60 minutes when your team can when your team focus the challenge. You can run the challenge for small team, or even for hundreds of people.

Step Two: Prepare a Kit for Each Team

Before the meeting, prepare the exact amounts of ingredients for each team, don’t waste the time and bore the teams by counting the spaghetti when everybody is waiting in the room. Feel free to adjust the numbers, but commonly the following ingredients are used:

  • 20 sticks of spaghetti
  • 1 yard of string
  • 1 yard of masking tape
  • 1 marshmallow

Make sure you also have the followings to help to instruct and judge the challenge:

  • Measuring tape
  • Stopwatch
  • Optionally music system

Step Three: Give the Instructions

Let the teams know about the rules, and goals of the challenge. Be clear, but no need to feel people frustrated about the rules:

  • Build the tallest free-standing structure
  • The whole marshmallow must be on top
  • Use as much ingredients as you need from your kit
  • Can break up the spaghetti, string, or tape
  • Challenge lasts for 18 minutes

Step Four: Start the Challenge

Start the stopwatch and if it’s not disturbing the teams start some music.

  • Walk around the Room
  • Remind the Teams of the Time
  • Call Out How the Teams are Doing

Step Five: Finish the Challenge

Once the clock runs out, ask everybody to finish the work and sit down so everyone can see what the other teams were doing.

  • Measure the structures
  • Identify the winning team
  • Wrap up with the lessons of the challenge
  • Childen usually outperform adults in creativity
  • Emphasize the importance of prototyping
